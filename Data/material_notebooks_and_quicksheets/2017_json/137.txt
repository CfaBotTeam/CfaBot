{"id": 137, "text": "Ethics can be described as a set of shared beliefs about what is good or acceptable\nbehavior and what is bad or unacceptable behavior. Ethics also refers to the study of\ngood and bad behavior. Ethical conduct has been described as behavior that follows\nmoral principles and is consistent with society's ethical expectations.\nEthical conduct has also been described as conduct that improves outcomes for\nstakeholders, who are people directly or indirectly affected by the conduct. Examples\nof stakeholders in the case of investment professionals include their clients, co-workers,\nemployers, and the investment profession as a whole. Some decisions may bring positive\nresults for you, but negative consequences for a stakeholder, such as a co-worker. Ethical\nconduct is behavior that balances your self-interest with the impact on others.\n"}
