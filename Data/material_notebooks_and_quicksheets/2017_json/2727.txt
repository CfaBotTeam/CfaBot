{"id": 2727, "text": "A cyclical firm is one whose earnings are highly dependent on the stage of the business\ncycle. These firms have high earnings volatility and high operating leverage. Their\nproducts are often expensive, non-necessities whose purchase can be delayed until\nthe economy improves. Examples of cyclical industries include basic materials and\nprocessing, consumer discretionary, energy, financial services, industrial and producer\ndurables, and technology.\n"}
