{"id": 3829, "text": "A zero-volatility spread or Z-spread is the percent spread that must be added to each spot\nrate on the benchmark yield curve to make the present value of a bond equal to its price.\nAn option-adjusted spread or OAS is used for bonds with embedded options. For a\ncallable bond, the OAS is equal to the Z-spread minus the call option value in basis\npoints.\n"}
