{"id": 1333, "text": "A Giffen good is an inferior good for which the negative income effect of a price\ndecrease outweighs the positive substitution effect, so that a decrease (increase) in the\ngood's price has a net result of decreasing (increasing) the quantity consumed.\nA Veblen good is also one for which an increase (decrease) in price results in an increase\n(decrease) in the quantity consumed. However, a Veblen good is not an inferior good\nand is not supported by the axioms of the theory of demand.\n"}
